The Research is Clear

Your Walls Are Already Teaching — The Question Is What

Research shows that carefully chosen images — such as nature-themed murals or child-centered artwork — significantly enhance creativity, reduce anxiety, and boost positivity in educational settings.

The landmark Holistic Evidence and Design (HEAD) study followed 3,766 students across 153 classrooms and found that visually stimulating classroom walls foster better learning outcomes — including in subjects like mathematics.

A note on surfaces: Standard painted cinder block walls — common in schools — print beautifully. Very rough or uneven surfaces may not be suitable; we assess your space during a free consultation.

"Well-designed primary school environments can boost learning progress in reading, writing, and math by an average of 16% in a single year."

— HEAD Study, University of Salford, UK
